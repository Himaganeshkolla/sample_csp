Symptoms

he rice blast fungus may infect and produce lesions on most of the shoot but usually not the leaf sheath. From the seedling stage through plant maturity, rice blast progresses through several phases starting with leaf blast, followed by collar, panicle and node blast

       tricyclazole (Systemic Fungicide)
       Effective against Rice Blast by inhibiting fungal growth.
       Commonly used in India and various other rice-growing regions.

       Propiconazole (Triazole Fungicide)
       Controls fungal diseases, including Rice Blast.
       Used as a preventive or curative treatment in rice fields.

       Azoxystrobin (Strobilurin Fungicide)
       Provides broad-spectrum disease control, including Rice Blast.
       Used in combination with other fungicides for better efficacy.
       
       Isoprothiolane
       Combats Rice Blast and suppresses the disease in rice plants.
       Applied to rice fields as a protective measure.
       
       Carbendazim (Benzimidazole Fungicide)
       Effective against a range of fungal diseases, including Rice Blast.
       Used in integrated pest management strategies.
